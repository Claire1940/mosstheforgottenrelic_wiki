<Section icon={Target} title="Beginner Guide for Quill and the Reader">

In this adventure, you play two distinct roles simultaneously. You control Quill, the brave mouse protagonist, for platforming, dodging, and sword combat. At the same time, you act as the Reader, a giant magical presence that can reach into the world to move heavy blocks, manipulate platforms, heal Quill, and control enemies.

Understanding how to coordinate these two characters is the key to solving environmental puzzles and surviving early combat encounters. 

| Role | Primary Functions | Key Interactions |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Quill** | Movement, combat, traversal | Running, jumping, climbing ledges, dodging, sword slashing |
| **The Reader** | Environmental manipulation, support | Dragging blocks, rotating pillars, healing Quill, stunning Arcane units |

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Separate Your Controls">
    Train your muscle memory to separate Quill's physical movements from the Reader's environmental manipulation. Use the left stick to guide Quill through platforms, and use the Reader's cursor to prepare pathways ahead of her.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Scan the Environment for Scratches">
    Look closely at ledges, walls, and pillars. Yellowish scratch marks indicate surfaces that Quill can climb or grab. If a platform has these markings but is out of reach, the Reader must slide or rotate it into place.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Build the Route Before Moving">
    Do not rush Quill into a room. Stop at the entrance, inspect the layout, and use the Reader to align blocks or raise platforms. Setting up the path beforehand minimizes accidental falls and resets.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Utilize Arcane Enemies as Tools">
    Do not defeat every beetle-like Arcane unit immediately. Many puzzles require you to use the Reader's power to grab an enemy and hold it down on a pressure plate or direct its projectile at a distant switch.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Combat Accessibility">
If you find the dual-character combat sections overwhelming, you can use the built-in Skip Combat option. This allows you to bypass intense battle rooms and focus entirely on the story and environmental puzzles.
</Callout>

</Section>
