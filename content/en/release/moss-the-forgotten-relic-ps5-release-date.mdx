<Section icon={Swords} title="Non-VR Gameplay & Controls Guide">

Transitioning a game from virtual reality to standard flat screens requires a massive overhaul of how the player interacts with the environment. In this edition, you continue to play a dual role: controlling **Quill**, the physical hero on the ground, and acting as the **Reader**, a mythical, giant presence capable of altering the world itself. 

On the PlayStation 5, the DualSense controller handles these dual roles simultaneously. The left analog stick governs Quill's movement, dodging, and sword combat. Meanwhile, the right analog stick or motion controls manipulate the Reader's cursor, allowing you to grab objects, heal Quill, and control enemies in real-time.

| Role | Core Controls | Environmental Interactions | Combat Utility |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Quill** | Left stick (Move), Cross/A (Jump), Square/X (Attack) | Climbing ledges, pushing lightweight objects, path traversal | Fast sword strikes, directional dodging, charged weapon attacks |
| **The Reader** | Right stick/Cursor, R2/RT (Grab/Interact) | Dragging heavy blocks, raising platforms, growing climbable ivy | Healing Quill, stunning Arcane enemies, triggering environmental traps |

<Callout type="tip" title="Dual-Control Mastery">
Keep a close eye on both characters during busy encounters. You can use the Reader to hold a threatening enemy in place while guiding Quill to strike them from behind, or drag a block to block incoming ranged projectiles.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Target} title="How to Start: Step-by-Step Progression">

Guiding Quill through her adventure requires a balance of platforming skill and environmental puzzle-solving. To ensure you experience the narrative in its intended sequence, follow this recommended progression path:

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Begin with Book I">
    While the main menu allows you to jump directly into Book II, starting with Book I is highly recommended. It establishes the bond between Quill and the Reader, introduces the core puzzle language, and builds the narrative foundation.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Master Dual-Character Coordination">
    Spend time in the early chapters of the Clearing and Mire Temple getting used to moving Quill with one hand while dragging blocks or activating switches with the Reader cursor.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Locate Twilight Garden Portals">
    As you progress through Book I, look out for glowing dimensional portals hidden in the Mire Temple, Last Respite, and the Abandoned City. These lead to standalone challenge rooms that reward powerful equipment.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Transition to Book II">
    After completing the final boss of Book I, load up Book II. This second half introduces advanced mechanics like weapon switching, Nature Attunement, and more aggressive enemy types.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Skip Combat Feature">
If you find a particular battle room or boss encounter too difficult, you can access the "Skip Combat" option from the pause menu. This allows you to progress the story without completing the fight, though you must still solve any environmental puzzles in that room.
