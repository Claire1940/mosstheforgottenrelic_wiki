<Section icon={Target} title="Dual-Control Mechanics: Guide for Quill and the Reader">

Succeeding in this fantasy world requires mastering the dual-character control system. You simultaneously direct Quill, a brave young mouse seeking to rescue her uncle, and the Reader, an ethereal guiding force capable of shifting the physical environment. Most rooms act as self-contained puzzle boxes where progress is impossible without tight coordination between both entities.

Quill handles local navigation, climbing, dodging, and direct sword combat. The Reader, represented by an on-screen magical cursor, interacts with heavy stone blocks, rotating platforms, and hostile Arcane forces. Learning when to switch your focus between the two is the key to surviving early-game hazards.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Separate Controls and Roles">
    Assign left-stick inputs to Quill's physical movement and right-stick inputs to the Reader's cursor. Practice moving Quill to a safe corner before using the Reader to align platforms or drag obstacles.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Analyze the Environment">
    Look for white scratch marks on ledges, walls, and pillars. These visual indicators point directly to climbable routes, hidden alcoves, or surfaces where blocks can be positioned.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Leverage Arcane Enemies">
    Do not immediately defeat every beetle-like Arcane unit. Use the Reader cursor to grab and stun these enemies, then drag them onto glowing pressure plates to open gates or lower bridges.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Combat encounters are designed to be fast but manageable. When Quill takes damage, her health can be restored by hovering the Reader's cursor over her and holding the interaction button. This heals her wound-by-wound, but leaves the Reader temporarily unable to manipulate environmental objects.

<Callout type="tip" title="Pacing Interaction">
Always clear a safe landing spot for Quill before attempting to move complex machinery. If a platform resets automatically when released, keep holding it with the Reader until Quill has safely crossed to the opposite side.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Boss Encounters and Combat Strategies">

Combat encounters in this collection demand tactical movement rather than button-mashing. Standard battle rooms pit Quill against mixed groups of Arcane and Forged enemies, including explosive Tickers, distant Scorchers, and heavily armored melee units. Boss encounters serve as grand puzzles where survival relies on manipulating the arena itself.

During boss fights, the Reader must actively monitor the entire screen to predict attack patterns, heal Quill, and operate defensive barriers.

| Boss Name | Campaign Phase | Primary Attack Patterns | Key Reader Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sarffog | Book I Finale | Launches fire sweeps, pressures small platforms | Operate escape routes, clear paths |
| The Warden | Book II Mid-Game | Slams heavy hammer, summons Forged minions | Restrain boss weapon, trigger traps |
| Tylan | Book II Finale | Performs ground pounds, aerial dives, chases | Charge weapons, pull bells to stun |

When facing standard enemies, prioritize ranged attackers first. Scorchers and Bombers can severely limit Quill's movement, making it easy for melee units to corner her. Use the Reader to grab explosive enemies and redirect their blasts into armored targets.

<Callout type="warning" title="Skip Combat Accessibility">
If a combat arena or boss encounter becomes too difficult, players can activate the Skip Combat prompt from the pause menu. This allows you to bypass the fight and continue the story, though you must still manually solve any environmental puzzles required to exit the room.
</Callout>
