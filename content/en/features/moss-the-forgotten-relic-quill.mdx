<Section icon={Target} title="moss the forgotten relic quill: Controls & Dual-Playstyle">

Navigating the world requires a solid grasp of how to split your attention between your two characters. While Quill handles the immediate platforming and swordplay, the Reader is responsible for lifting heavy blocks, turning gears, and keeping enemies at bay. This cooperative dynamic is the core of the game's design, and mastering it early prevents unnecessary restarts.

The control scheme has been rebuilt to feel natural on standard inputs. On a controller, the left stick guides Quill's physical movements, while the right stick controls the Reader's magical cursor. On PC, players can utilize standard WASD controls alongside precise mouse cursor movements.

| Action | Controller Input | Keyboard & Mouse Input | Mechanic Details |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Quill Move** | Left Thumbstick | WASD Keys | Direct movement, climbing, and platforming |
| **Quill Attack** | Face Button (X/Square) | Left Click | Fast sword swings, combos, and charged strikes |
| **Quill Dodge** | Face Button (A/Cross) | Spacebar | Evade incoming melee strikes and projectile zones |
| **Reader Cursor** | Right Thumbstick | Mouse Movement | Hover over interactive objects and Arcane units |
| **Reader Interact** | Trigger Button (RT/R2) | Right Click | Grab, slide, rotate blocks, heal Quill, hold enemies |

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Start with Book I">
    Begin your adventure by selecting Book I from the main menu. This introductory campaign slowly teaches you the interactive language of the world, allowing you to get comfortable with dual-character controls before the puzzles and combat increase in complexity.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Separate Your Inputs">
    Practice moving Quill with your left hand while aiming the Reader's cursor with your right hand. Try running across a bridge while simultaneously rotating a platform ahead of you to build muscle memory.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Read the Environment">
    Look closely at ledges, walls, and pillars. Yellowish scratch marks and worn paint indicate surfaces that Quill can grab, climb, or jump between. If a ledge lacks these markings, it is generally out of reach.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Build the Route First">
    Before sending Quill rushing into a new room, pause and study the layout. Identify movable blocks, pressure plates, and gates. Position your platforms and blocks ahead of time to minimize platforming errors.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Combat Healing">
When Quill takes damage, her health does not regenerate automatically. To heal her, guide the Reader cursor over Quill and hold the interaction input. This channels a soothing blue light that restores her health. Always dodge to a safe corner before attempting to heal.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Environmental Puzzle Solutions">

Puzzles in Moss: The Forgotten Relic require a cooperative effort between Quill's physical agility and the Reader's environmental manipulation. If you find yourself stuck in a room, the best approach is to identify your final destination, locate the mechanism connected to it, and work backward to find the starting trigger.

Many puzzles utilize the beetle-like Arcane enemies as interactive tools. Instead of defeating every threat immediately, observe their behavior to see if they can assist you in clearing the room.

| Puzzle Type | Core Mechanic | Primary Solution | Pro Tip |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Movable Block** | Object dragging | Drag blocks under marked scratched ledges | Adjust block close to ledge for easier climbing |
| **Rotating Platform** | Mechanism control | Hold and rotate with Reader while Quill crosses | Keep holding mechanism if it resets automatically |
| **Pressure Plate** | Weight activation | Drag an Arcane enemy onto the switch plate | Keep holding enemy with Reader so it cannot move |
| **Hidden Path** | Reveal interaction | Use reveal ability on marked magical areas | Backtrack to early areas once reveal is unlocked |
| **Vine Growth** | Nature Attunement | Grow climbable ivy to connect vertical walls | Connect roots across gaps to open new pathways |

<Callout type="info" title="Arcane Control">
The Reader can grab and control certain beetle-like Arcane enemies. Once held, you can drag them onto pressure plates to keep gates open or aim their ranged attacks at distant switches that Quill cannot reach.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Boss Strategies and Combat Optimization">

Combat encounters in the game emphasize positioning, evasion, and teamwork over button-mashing. Quill is small and can easily be overwhelmed by groups of Arcane and Forged forces. Use directional dodges to avoid red warning markers, and use the Reader to crowd-control dangerous targets.

Boss fights serve as grand spectacles that test everything you have learned. Each boss features distinct phases that require a mix of platforming, weapon switching, and environmental manipulation.

| Boss Name | Main Encounter | Key Attack Pattern | Counterstrategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sarffog** | Book I Finale | Launches fiery breath across Quill's path | Wait for fire to pass, move quickly between cover |
| **The Warden** | Book II Foundry | Slams fists, releases expanding heat waves | Dodge impact markers, jump over expanding heat waves |
| **Tylan** | Book II Pinnacle | Ground-pound attacks, aerial dives, chases | Use Hammer Trap, hit bell with Reader, attack when stunned |

<Callout type="warning" title="Skip Combat Accessibility">
If you find a combat arena or boss encounter too difficult, the game offers a Skip Combat option. When the prompt appears, you can choose to bypass the fight and continue the story. Note that enemies required for solving puzzles will still need to be managed.
</Callout>
