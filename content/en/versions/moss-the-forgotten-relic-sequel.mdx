<Section icon={Shield} title="Beginner Guide: Dual-Control System">

Playing without a VR headset introduces a unique dual-control layout. You must guide Quill, the brave mouse protagonist, while simultaneously acting as the Reader, a giant magical presence that can manipulate the environment. This cooperative dynamic is central to both environmental puzzles and combat encounters.

Managing both roles requires a clear division of focus. On standard controllers, the left analog stick and face buttons govern Quill's movement, jumps, dodges, and sword swings. The right analog stick controls the Reader's cursor, allowing you to grab objects, heal Quill, and restrain enemies. Learning to coordinate these inputs is essential for surviving the increasingly difficult challenges of the sequel campaign.

Book II introduces larger dioramas, more intricate multi-stage puzzles, and formidable boss encounters that require you to utilize all of Quill's upgraded gear.

<Section icon={Swords} title="Sequel Weapons and Reader Abilities">

The sequel campaign, *Moss: Book II*, significantly expands Quill's arsenal. While the first game relies almost entirely on basic sword combat, the sequel introduces specialized weapons and Reader-assisted traversal powers. These tools are not just for defeating enemies; they are also used to solve environmental puzzles and reveal hidden paths during backtracking.

Each weapon features a unique charged ability that requires teamwork. By holding the weapon button, the Reader can reach down and charge Quill's equipped tool with magical energy. This unlocks devastating attacks and traversal techniques, such as dashing across wide chasms or throwing a spinning blade to hit distant switches.

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Collectibles and Hidden Secrets Guide">

For completionists, the collection offers a wealth of hidden secrets spread across both campaigns. Finding these items rewards you with beautiful stained-glass artwork, lore details, and powerful optional armor sets that change Quill's appearance.

Forgotten Fragments appear as glowing scroll pieces hidden on high ledges, behind foreground scenery, or along optional side paths. Relic Dust is obtained by smashing decorative containers like barrels, pots, and crates. Accumulating enough Relic Dust allows you to open the sealed doors of the three Relic Shrines, which house the game's ultimate armor rewards.
