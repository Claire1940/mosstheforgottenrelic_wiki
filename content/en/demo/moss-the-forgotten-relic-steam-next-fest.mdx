<Section icon={Target} title="Beginner Guide: Mastering the Dual-Control System">

The most unique aspect of **moss the forgotten relic** is the dual-control system. Unlike traditional action-adventures where you control a single avatar, you are responsible for both **Quill**, the mouse hero, and the **Reader**, a giant spectral presence that interacts with the world.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Understand Your Roles">
    Quill handles the tactical movement: running, jumping, dodging, and swordplay. The Reader handles the environmental manipulation: moving heavy blocks, rotating platforms, and slowing down enemies.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Scan the Environment">
    Before moving Quill, use the Reader's cursor to highlight objects. If an object glows or has blue magical particles, the Reader can grab or pull it. Look for scratch marks on ledges to identify climbable paths.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Coordinate Combat">
    In battle, use Quill to strike enemies while using the Reader to stun or hold dangerous foes. This is especially vital when facing multiple Arcane units at once.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Master Healing">
    The Reader can heal Quill by reaching out and interacting with her directly. Always create a safe distance between Quill and enemies before initiating the heal, as it takes a few seconds to complete.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Input Tip">
While mouse-and-keyboard support is fully implemented, the developers recommend a controller for the most fluid experience when managing simultaneous character movements.
</Callout>

</Section>

**Common Puzzle Obstacles:**
- **Pressure Plates**: Often require an Arcane enemy to be dragged and held on the plate by the Reader.
- **Light Mirrors**: Found in late-game Book II; requires rotating mirrors in sequence to reflect a beam into a receiver.
- **Sword Dash**: Use the Reader to charge Quill's sword, allowing her to dash across gaps that are too wide for a standard jump.

<Callout type="tip" title="Puzzle Strategy">
If you are stuck, work backward from the exit door. Identify what triggers the final gate, then find the mechanism that powers that trigger. Most solutions involve moving a block or an enemy to a specific spot.
</Callout>
